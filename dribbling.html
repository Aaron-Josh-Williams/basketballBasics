LEARN TO DRIBBLE

Since the dribbler is the player with the ball, they are the most important player at that moment. The dribbler has the power to make a play happen and has to make the decision on what to do. The dribbler should become a triple threat to the defender, meaning they can pass, dribble, or shoot at any moment to make that defender's job more difficult.

Dribbling Rules

  • You can dribble anywhere on the court as long as you stay inbounds
  • You must only use one hand at a time (violation is called a double dribble)
  • Your hand must stay on top of the ball. Once you pick up your dribble, you cannot dribble again (violation is also called adouble dribble)
  • You can only take two steps once you pick up your dribble (violation is called a travel)

Dribbling Drills

    1: Pound dribble knee high

  • Keep knees bent the whole time
  • Ball should come up to your knee on each dribble
  • Pound ball as hard as you can
  • Keep looking forward
  • Ball should touch finger pads on each dribble

    2: Half circle dribble

  • Twist body right and left while dribbling ball as far as you can in the same direction your twisting
  • Keep the ball low
  • Avoid slapping the ball
  • Keep looking forward

    3: Cone touch

  • Spread 10 cones out and touch as many cones as you can before time runs out
  • May come back to a cone after touching at least two others
  • Go as fast as you can
  • Don't pick up your dribble
